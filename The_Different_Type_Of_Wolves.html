The Different Type of Wolves

As you read along you will learn the 7 different wolves that are roaming the earth.

Gray Wolf

Gray Wolves are the most commonly know around the world. Gray Wolves are usually found in deep forests but there have been some spotting of gray wolves in washington. The Gray Wolf, another name for it Canis Lupus, is short for Dog(Canis) and Wolf(Lupus) in latin. The difference between dog dna and wolf dna is by (0.2%). But the Gray Wolf has been exterminated in many parts of North America. When you hear wolves howl thats a sign of communication among with other wolf packs. Something else is that wolves can reach a speed of 40 mph when chasing something.

Red Wolf

Red Wolves are not really red despite their name, they have more of a brownish coat to them but a little of red behind their ears. Red Wolf puppies are born in litters of 2-8 pups. So Red Wolf pups are protected from their other pack memebers until they hit maturity and then they wonder off and create their own pack. Red Wolves are Critically Endangered so they were put on a breeding program where many more came to life and was released into the wild. Red Wolves actually have no natural predators like mountain lions or something like that. Red Wolves usually bond for life with their loved one.

Indian Wolf

Indian Wolves are usually found in India, Pakistan, Afghanistan, Nepal, Bhutan, Israel, Turkey, Iran, and Syria. There are about 10,000 alive right now. But Indian Wolves can still go extinct by habitat loss, unregulated hunting, and loss of prey. These 3 things are crucial for Indian Wolves. Heres a little fun fact for kids. Indian Wolves are know for preying on children. In fact it got it's own name called "Child-Lifting." The Wolves in the Jungle Book are actually Indian Wolves.

Himalayan Wolf

Himalayan Wolves are actually desendents of the gray wolf. These Wolves are usually found in Jammu and Kashmir, Nepal, China, Mongolia and the Himalayas(Hint Hint). Himalayan Wolves usually pack up to a group of 6-8. Unlike Red Wolves when it comes to territory though, Himalayan Wolves tend to be passive when invading their space. Humans are the natural threat to Himalayan Wolves and causes their population to decrease. These wolves were listed as Endangered but with some help from the breeding program they was able to successfully bring the population of the Himalayan Wolf back up

Ethiopian Wolf

Ethiopian Wolves also known as the "ky kebero" meaning red jackal is known around Afro-alpine regions of Ethiopia and Eritrea. Thats 10,000 feet above sea level. BUt in those regions they tend to live in open moorlands where vegetation is than 0.25 meters high. These Wolves are extreamly endangered with only 450 left in the world. Where 300 of them live in the areas listed before. Loss of habitat has been a big one for the Ethiopia Wolves. Because of Farmers they had to keep moving homes. IN a pack it contains about 12 wolves and 1 female for each 7th wolf.

Eastern Wolf

Eastern Wolves lives in deciduous forests in Eastern America but also live in Ontario and Quebec but mostly live in Algonquin Provincial Park. Because of deforestation though the number of Eastern Wolves has gotten smaller. These wolves are in danger because of hunting and trapping. These wolves has been marked as Threatened. The biggest threats they are facing though is inter-breeding with coyotes and habitat loss.

Artic Wolf

The Artic Wolf also known as the "Canis lupus arctos" is quite famous among the northern edge of North America and along the north side of the north pole and the eastern and northern shores of Greenland. Some of the threats of the Artic Wolf is Polar Bears, Other Wolves, and Hunters. they usually eat a lot of other things like others wolves but something that they do is eat their own kind. Artic Wolves live in -30°F(-22°C). They can survive 7 years of these harsh conditions. They either live alone or in a pack of 6. Something else is that Artic Wolves tend to be smaller that Gray Wolves in a lot of their features like their ears are smaller and more.
